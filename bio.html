Biography of Jheanelle Owens

Early Years

Jheanelle was born in Kingston, Jamaica at UWI hospital. She grew up in the countryside of St. Catherine, Jamaica where she got up early each morning to commute to school. Her preparatory studies began at St. Hugh's Prep school, where she passed her GSAT exams and was admitted to the Immaculate Conception High School.

Teenage Years

At Immaculate, Jheanelle joined the ICHS symphony orchestra and developed her skills as a violinist and musician. She eventually went on to become the longest serving concert master of that group until she graduated. She accomplished distinctions in all ten of her CSEC subjects, most notably placing first in Jamaica and third in the Caribbean for Additional Mathematics. For the CAPE examinations, Jheanelle obtained 4 distinctions in 12th grade and 5 distinctions in 13th grade, including top placements in Pure Mathematics and Applied Mathematics.

Early Adulthood

Jheanelle was accepted into Tufts University in the summer of 2017, and attended the institution to complete her undergraduate studies in Computer Science. While the CS curriculum equiped her with valuable modern day skills, the classes she took in Africana studies allowed her to explore her history and gain an understanding of the world. She combined these two aspects of her curriculum to design and teach a class alongside Tony Goss in the Tufts Experimental College. She taught "Techquity" to her fellow undergraduates in the spring semesters of her junior and senior year. Jheanelle's anticipated graduation date is August 14, 2021.
